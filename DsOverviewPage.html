Nanopublishing in Data Science

The Data Science journal recommends that papers come with their main (and other) findings represented in a machine-understandable way as nanopublications.

For that, first create and publish your nanopublications here, and then link them from your new submission or existing manuscript at the journal website.

Supported Types of Nanopublications

  • General Super-Pattern Statements: Such statements are advanced and logically precise scientific statements. Difficulty: advanced
  • Simple Causal/Spatio-Temporal/Comparative Relations: Such statements state a relation between two individuals. Difficulty: medium
  • Class Definitions: Such statements define a new class. Difficulty: low
  • Definition of Individual: Such statements define a new individual as an instance of a class. Difficulty: low
