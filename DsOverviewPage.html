Nanopublishing in Data Science

The Data Science journal recommends that papers come with their findings represented in a machine-understandable way as nanopublications.

This site guides you to create and publish your nanopublications, which you can then link from your new submission or existing manuscript at the journal website.

Choose Nanopublication Types

These nanopublication types are supported by the journal to express your scientific claims:

  • Simple Scientific Relations between Instances establish a general causal (e.g. A caused B), spatio-temporal (e.g. A is included in B), or comparative (e.g. A is larger than B) relation between two individuals (not classes), for example that the invention of the telephone was necessary for the Internet. Difficulty: medium
  • Simple Scientific Relations based on Classes state a general causal/spatio-temporal/comparative relation as above, but based on classes rather than individuals. For example, we can state that instances of the class smoking tend to have the relation of causing to instances of the class lung cancer. Such statements are less precise than the super-pattern statements below, as the aspect of "tending to" is underspecified. Difficulty: medium
  • General Super-Pattern Statements are advanced and logically precise scientific statements, such as "whenever a person has a headache then this is mostly (>50%) caused by a condition of dehydration of that person". Difficulty: advanced
  • Coming soon: AIDA Statements use English sentences to make statements formally linkable without having to formally specify the statements themselves. If the above types do not seem feasible for you, AIDA statements might be a good option. Difficulty: low to medium
  • Coming soon: Life Science Statements from Free Text (at external tool Knowledge Collaboratory) are automatically suggested formal statements based on free text you write down or paste in. They only work on Life Science topics at the moment. Difficulty: low to medium

These types allow you to introduce and describe your new approach, software, or dataset:

  • Coming soon: Approach Introductions describe a new scientific approach. Difficulty: low
  • Coming soon: Software Introductions describe a new piece of software of scientific relevance. Difficulty: low
  • Coming soon: Dataset Introductions describe a new scientific dataset. Difficulty: low

You can also define new classes and instances:

  • Class Definitions declare a new class, such as operant research as a subclass of research. Difficulty: low
  • Definitions of Individuals declare a new individual as an instance of a class, for example Pluto as an instance of the class dwarf planet. Difficulty: low

If you can't find a type that works for your claims, tell us by opening a support ticket.
